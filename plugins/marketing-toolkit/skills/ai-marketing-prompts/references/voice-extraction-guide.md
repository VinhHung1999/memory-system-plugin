# Voice Extraction Guide

How to describe your brand voice in a way AI can actually apply.

## Step 1: Collect 5-10 best-performing past samples

Pull copy that worked — landing page hero, top-performing social posts, email subject lines that hit above benchmark, customer testimonials you've quoted.

## Step 2: Extract 3-5 voice adjectives

Ask: if our brand were a person, what 3-5 words would describe them?

Examples:
- **Tech SaaS:** "confident, data-driven, contrarian, dry-humored"
- **D2C wellness:** "warm, scientific, inclusive, patient"
- **B2B consultancy:** "authoritative, no-BS, pragmatic, senior-to-senior"
- **Creator/personal brand:** "curious, vulnerable, direct, playful"

## Step 3: Define anti-voice

What your brand is NOT. AI needs this to avoid defaults.

Examples:
- NOT: hyped, buzzword-heavy, exclamation-point-laden, jargon-dense
- NOT: overly formal, corporate-speak, hedge-everything, generic positive

## Step 4: Write 2-3 voice do/don't rules

Concrete rules AI can follow:

**Example rules for "friendly, educational, earnest" (plant shop):**
- DO: use specific plant species names (Philodendron Gloriosum, not "rare plant")
- DO: give care tips alongside any product mention
- DO: acknowledge when a plant is hard to grow — honesty builds trust
- DON'T: use urgency language ("limited time!", "hurry!") — it breaks earnestness
- DON'T: use emojis except occasional 🌱 — we're not that brand
- DON'T: talk down to beginners — respect their learning curve

## Step 5: Pass samples + adjectives + rules to AI

Template:
```
**Brand voice (R):**
Adjectives: {your 3-5 adjectives}

Anti-voice (what we're NOT): {anti adjectives}

Rules:
- DO: {rule 1}
- DO: {rule 2}
- DON'T: {rule 3}
- DON'T: {rule 4}

Past samples that hit the voice (for you to pattern-match):
---
{sample 1}
---
{sample 2}
---
{sample 3}
```

## Ongoing voice refinement

Every time AI produces something that's off-voice, ask yourself:
- What specifically felt wrong?
- Was it missing from my rules list?
- Add the new rule for next time.

Over 10-20 iterations, your voice doc becomes a reliable style transfer.

## Tools that help

- **TomoLogic, Brand24, Writer.com** — tools that can codify voice into a profile
- **Paste + prompt**: simplest approach, works across ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini
- **Custom GPT / Claude Project**: set voice as system instructions, reuse

## When AI can't match your voice

Some voices are genuinely hard for AI:
- **Highly specific cultural insider voice** (subculture-native)
- **Unique sentence rhythms** (deliberately fragmentary, poetic)
- **Niche humor** (satire, absurdism, industry in-jokes)

For these: use AI for drafting, then always do heavy human rewrite for voice. Or only use AI for non-voice tasks (metrics analysis, clustering, not creative copy).
