# Email Type ↔ Funnel Stage Matrix

From Google's Digital Marketing certificate, Course 4 Module 2.

## The matrix

| Email type | Primary funnel stage | Goal | Content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | Awareness | Bring net-new customers in | Introduce brand/product to cold or warmed-but-unconverted leads |
| **Welcome** | Consideration | Onboard opted-in subscribers | Reaffirm value prop, set expectations, first action |
| **Newsletter** | Consideration / Conversion / Loyalty | Maintain relationship | Regular curated content, mix of edu + promo + community |
| **Promotional** | Consideration / Loyalty | Drive purchases | Offer-driven, single focus, urgency/scarcity |
| **Retention** | Loyalty | Keep existing customers active | Win-back lapsed users, replenishment, loyalty perks |

## How to pick

**Acquisition** — when you have email addresses from lead magnets, partnerships, or ad campaigns, and the people haven't engaged yet. Goal is to warm them up. Tone is introductory, brand-heavy. Don't pitch hard.

**Welcome** — immediately after signup/opt-in. Highest open rates of any email type (often 50%+). Use this as the first impression: reaffirm why they signed up, what they'll get, and give one clear next action (profile complete, first purchase discount, resource download).

**Newsletter** — regular cadence (weekly/biweekly/monthly). Serves every funnel stage — that's why mix matters. Default mix:
- 40% educational (tips, industry insights)
- 30% entertaining (behind-the-scenes, humor, community highlights)
- 20% curated (links to best-of content)
- 10% promotional

**Promotional** — event-driven (sale, launch, holiday). Single offer, single CTA. Should be rare enough to be exciting but frequent enough to be expected. Don't overuse or subscribers will tune out.

**Retention** — scheduled based on behavior triggers:
- 30 days no engagement → "we miss you" + soft offer
- 60 days no purchase → stronger incentive (discount, free shipping)
- 90 days inactive → "last chance" or sunset flow (remove from list if no response)
- Replenishment: calculate avg time between purchases, email at 0.8× that interval

## Common mistakes

- **Sending a newsletter to cold leads** — they haven't opted in to regular contact, so you'll get spam flags. Use acquisition instead.
- **Sending promotional to new welcome-flow subscribers** — breaks the trust the welcome email is building. Wait 2-3 emails into the welcome sequence before first promo.
- **Calling everything a "newsletter"** — if the content is event-driven (sale, launch), it's promotional. Don't dilute the newsletter brand with one-off blasts.
- **Skipping the welcome email** — foundational mistake. Welcome emails have 4-5× the open/CTR of any other type. Never let a subscriber go unwelcomed.
