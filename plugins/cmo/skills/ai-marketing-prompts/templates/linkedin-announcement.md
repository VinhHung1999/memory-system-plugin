# LinkedIn / Social Announcement Prompt Template

From Course 7 M4 m4_09 "Congratulations" — use AI to rewrite a draft in 3 tones so you can pick what fits.

Use for: company milestone announcements, personal career news, product launches, hiring posts.

## Full T-C-R-E-I prompt

```
**Task (T):** Take my draft LinkedIn post and rewrite it in 3 different tones. I'll pick the one that fits the moment.

**Context (C):**
- What I'm announcing: {announcement_summary}
- Why it matters to my audience: {relevance}
- My role: {founder / marketer / employee / personal}
- Expected audience: {followers_description}
- Goal: {pure_awareness / recruit_applicants / drive_signups / get_reactions}

**References (R):**
My draft:
```
{paste_your_draft}
```

**Evaluate (E):**
Rewrite in these 3 tones, each labeled clearly:

1. **Humble** — Acknowledge team + customers, downplay self, thank-you-heavy. Risk: may undersell.
2. **Confident** — Lead with the accomplishment + what it proves. Direct, numbers-forward. Risk: may feel braggy.
3. **Story-led** — Open with a scene/moment that sets up the announcement. Narrative arc. Risk: may delay the point.

For each version, include:
- Estimated length (characters)
- Likely best reaction type (comments / reposts / DMs)
- Risk flag if going too far in that direction

**Iterate (I):**
After the 3 versions:
- "Pick the one most likely to get replies (not just likes). Tighten by 20%."
- "Take the best version and rewrite for Twitter/X (280 chars max)"
- "Add a strong hook to the first line that works even if someone doesn't open to read more"
```

## Why 3 tones instead of asking for the "best"

"Best" is subjective and AI defaults to average. Asking for 3 distinct tones:
- Surfaces the range of what's possible
- You pick based on the moment (humble for team win, confident for hard-fought, story-led for emotional)
- Teaches you what tone suits your voice over time

## LinkedIn-specific format rules

When iterating, enforce LinkedIn best practices:
- 1200-2000 chars for organic reach
- Line breaks every 1-2 sentences
- First 210 chars visible before "see more" click — hook must be in that window
- End with a question to drive comments
- Hashtags: 3-5 industry-focused

## Common failure modes

- **All 3 tones sound the same** → Brand voice too dominant. Ask: "Version 1 MUST be self-deprecating. Version 2 MUST open with a number. Version 3 MUST start with dialogue or a scene."
- **Hashtag spam** → "Max 5 hashtags, all industry-specific, no #motivation #inspiration generic stuff."
- **Emoji overload** → "Max 1 emoji, only if it punctuates a serious point."
